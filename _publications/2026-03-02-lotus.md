excerpt: "Solving large two-stage stochastic mixed-integer programs is computationally challenging. We propose LOTUS, a subset-based warm-start framework that enhances Dual Decomposition under fixed time budgets. By initializing the dual search with informed multipliers, LOTUS accelerates primal convergence and partially alleviates the impact of weak LP relaxations. Through an extensive computational study on production planning instances, we show that, within two hours, LOTUS yields significantly better primal solutions in 45.83% of cases, while being outperformed by Dual Decomposition in only 4.17%."
